In the late 19th century one Julius Sterling Morton was a newspaper editor and politician who wound up the Secretary of Agriculture under Grover Cleveland. Julius loved trees and actually founded Arbor Day in America.

But he also had several kids, among them Joy Morton and Mark Morton. Joy and Mark founded many companies together: Morton Lumber, Morton Sand and Gravel, Morton Salt. Heady stuff.

Later on Mark went to work for a refrigeration / meat-packing company, Western Cold Storage.

One day a young engineer named Frank Pearne asked Joy Morton to help fund his fledgeling teleprinter project. This was the age of telegraphy. Joy contacted his brother who asked one of Western Cold Storage's engineers, Charles Krum, to help out. Various developments, experiments and patents ensued, including the appearance of Charles' newly-graduated son Howard Krum, but when the dust settled a new company was founded: _Morkrum_.

Morkrum got all the patents. Morkrum made all the original devices. For twenty years, buying a teleprinter meant buying a Morkrum. Western Union bought them. The Associated Press. The Post Office. Everyone. Finally, in 1928, perhaps sensing the commercial limits of the name, they renamed the company to Teletype (which they trademarked). Two years later they sold it to Western Electric, the manufacturing arm of AT&T.

So anyways, this is my petition: if you are starting some niche hobby OS project, please do yourself and all your users a favour and rename "tty" to "morkrum".
