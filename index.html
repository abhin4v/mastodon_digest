Workity Work: Software Bug Edition

I'm not sure I've found a bug in one of our acquired codes, a commercial version of something developed for/by the NRC. I do know I've found a fairly significant error in the documentation but without the source code, I have to do some forensic engineering to find out if it's a real problem or not.

I might go into the details later because it's an interesting (to me) investigation into primary sources and the development of physical correlations (basically generalized curve-fits to experimental data).

We have two codes that (coarsely) model aerosol transport and deposition within rooms and buildings. They both calculate the same aerosol removal correlation. One of them appears to use a constant that's 7 times larger than the other, 7 times larger than the canonical published reference from 1985.

So what? Well, this particular code was acquired as a 'basic component' from a vendor with an NQA-1 qualified QA program. That's like if you bought nuclear-grade pipe or a valve or a spool of cable - the purchaser (us) does not have to independently verify the item meets all its quality requirements. We just have to ensure that it went through a QA process equivalent to the one we'd use if we bought the part off eBay or AliExpress or wrote the software ourselves and wanted to use it in a safety-related application. The code comes pre-qualified and there's a whole pile of federal regulation that covers vendor responsibilities and non-conforming material. For software, this means a very formal bug handling process.
