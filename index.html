I wrote a script to install/update MicroHs (a small Haskell compiler) and Godwit (my Tidal-ish pattern language thing), because it's boring to keep doing the fair number of steps by hand:

https://mathr.co.uk/web/godwit.html#Bootstrap

Takes about 6mins on desktop, 45mins on tablet, haven't tried on phone yet. Doesn't including the once-only step of adding

```
if [ -d "$HOME/.mcabal/bin" ] ; then
PATH="$HOME/.mcabal/bin:$PATH"
fi
```

to ~/.profile or ~/.bashrc or similar...

Then to start making sounds you need classic Dirt(*) sampler with some samples:

(cd dirt && ./dirt ) &
cd godwit
make HC=mhs scheduler &
make HC=mhs repl
:set +l
hush
s [bd]

The first time you run the scheduler it spits out warnings about missing shared library until a pattern is entered in the repl, which is a bit distracting, I should fix that. To stop exit the repl with :q and then enter fg, ctrl-c, fg, ctrl-c to quit the scheduler and dirt.

#Haskell #MicroHs #TidalCycles #Godwit #LiveCoding

(*) 1/2

how to make haiku in English in the most boring possible way:
- say anything at all with 17 syllables

how to make haiku in English that feel like haiku and can be interesting:

mix as many as you want of the following elements:

- three "verses", short-long-short, grouped into two asymetric "lines" divided by a cesura: either short;long-short or short-long; short.
- an interjection or punctuation separating the "lines"
- an imagistic, sensorial voice, drawing attention to perceptions in a here-now, like painting a little sketch.
- animistic sensibility.
- juxtaposing two things, and letting the reader imagine why they're put together.
- at least one expression that situates the haiku in a cycle of seasons and yearly events. circular time.
- a feeling of incompleteness, of unresolvedness; a vignette rather than a short story. (haiku is originally the opening of a full poem, standing alone.)
