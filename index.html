I just remembered a bit of #etymology I learned years ago. It’s about the origins of the words ‘Lord’ and ‘lady’.

In old English ‘Lord’ was ‘hlaf ward’:

Hlaf = bread (loaf)
Ward = keeper

Over time ‘hlaf watd’ got contracted to ‘hlafward’ then ‘hlavard’ and then ‘Lord’ (there may or may not have been further changes).

In old English ‘Lady’ was ‘hlaf diege’:

Hlaf = bread
Diege = maker

Again over time ’hlaf diege’ got contracted to ‘hlafdiege’ then ‘hlafdy’ and the ‘Lady’ (again there may or may not have been further changes).

Of course, I stand to be corrected but this is my recollection of how it came about.
