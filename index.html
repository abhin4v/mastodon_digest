In quantum mechanics, particles are also waves. And this explains momentum! The 'momentum' of a particle is just how many times its wave wiggles per distance. More precisely: momentum is the number of wavelengths per meter, times Planck's constant.

In quantum mechanics, waves are also particles. So when you have atoms wiggling in a crystal, the waves they make are called 'phonons'. And they have momentum.

But there's a subtlety! When the atoms are evenly spaced, you can fit different sine waves to their motion, as shown here. So vibrations moving through a crystal have momentum... but the momentum is not a specific number any more. If you add the right number to the momentum you get a new wave that looks just the same!

This idea is familiar among people who study crystals and transistors and such - it's called "crystal momentum":

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystal_momentum

If you explain this stuff to a mathematician, they will start excitedly talking about how the Pontryagin dual of the integers is a circle. That's their way of saying that momentum of waves in a crystal lattice is only defined modulo Planck's constant divided by the distance between atoms. But Pontryagin duality is more general:

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontryagin_duality

Follow @j_bertolotti for cool animations like this!

@collectifission good question!

In a nutshell and from my biased perspective:

Substitoot is a browser extension that fetches missing replies when you are looking at the post. As such it can be super targeted (it’ll never fetch replies you aren’t interested in), but relies on you using chrome or Firefox.

FediFetcher is a server side script that attempts to guess which replies you might be interested in, and fetch these proactively. As such you can use it with any client (I hardly ever use the web browser), but also means it’ll occasionally not fetch replies you’d like to see, and it’ll fetch plenty of replies you’ll never look at.

IMO if you use only chrome or Firefox, Substitoot is the better option.

If you want to use any other client you’ll need to use FediFetcher.
