Food, not vegan, thoughts, ‘long form cooking’

I really enjoy what I call ‘long form cooking’. Obviously only when I have the time. I don’t, not much, anymore.

How I do it:

- I go out of my way to go to a fishmonger or butcher to get something more special than what is usually at a normal grocery store
- A whole fish, for example. Today I got a whole rainbow trout from Sun Fat. And also mussels, scallops
- I actually enjoy scaling, cleaning and gutting fish (don’t ask), so I do that

Meantime I’m also processing the mussels.

- I make fillets: I put them into a salt brine. Later, I will smoke them in my wok (wok smoking is a thing)
- with the trout bones; I make a ‘fumet’ (a type of fish stock)
- I sweat aromatics (onion, garlic, celery etc) in a pot with butter
- Deglaze with shaoxing wine
- Add the fish stock I just made
- Adjust the flavors (with crushed lemongrass stalks, Thai red chillies, lime leaves, curry leaves)
- Add the mussels and other seafood until ready

Then I have that seafood stew that I enjoy.

With the trout that I will smoke later, I will eat it with rice or make a smoked trout dip. Or maybe I’ll make some kind of sushi.

I give some bits of raw trout to my cat.

Should everyone cook like this? Probably not. I don’t think I can do this regularly.

But there is something about feeding myself in a way that takes effort, that isn’t just taking food out of a box, that rejuvenates me.

It’s my special interest.

As an immigrant it also helps me rebel against the ‘food out of a box’ way of eating in this country, that makes me feel disconnected from my food. I want to see whole fish; whole chickens, not stuff on a tray in a fridge or freezer.

It’s important to me. I have many memories of doing things like this with my grandparents and my parents. Even though I didn’t cook as much back ‘home’ (I didn’t have to), I now cook all of the things that make me remember what home tastes like.

#Food #cooking

i think it is a pretty good idea in any variety of homotopy type theory to add a "preferred transport function" (over equations of maps in the base category, not objects) to your definition of displayed category, of course along with a coherence making the type of (transport functions, coherences) contractible.

if you can already believe this, the interesting part as a formaliser is the cost: doing this to the 1Lab was a +697 -512 diff:

  • 6 for the new fields, because our code is spacious;
  • 65 for a new pair of helpers in constructing displayed categories (one to use the canonical transport functions, one if the morphism part of your displayed category is props);
  • 20 of lemmas connecting the "preferred" transport function with subst and with PathP;
  • 79 of unrelated code defining the category of sections of a fibration, because i'm bad at git;
  • 9 actual new lines of preferred transport function;
  • ±512 lines of mostly changing to-pathp to to-pathp[] i.e. renaming things to use the new lemmas.

if you're curious why anyone would, this is because many displayed categories that arise in practice (e.g. fundamental fibration) have displayed morphisms that break down as "some relevant data + some propositions", such that only the propositions need to be transported.

but in cubical, while you can compute the embedding into the "relevant parts" on a transport along a neutral path, you pick up cruft that ranges between "an amount of transport refls that a tactic can snap away" and "enough formal transports to have Agda OOM-killed"; and in book HoTT your entire embedding will just be stuck on the stuck transport, so you still need something like a tactic to move them out of the way of your embedding.

now of course if you were working directly on the fundamental fibration, e.g. to show that it is a fibration, you don't have to transport very often. but if you pull it back along some functor now every single composition, and the identities, have a transport attached. so now even if you're doing something very concrete (like normalisation by evaluation using gluing!) you're kinda boned because all your computations will probably be surrounded by mountains of cruft.

(and I'm pretty sure that in this case, even with strict β for J, no amount of carefully preserving refl (e.g. by having everything be eta records, and having your natural transformations have "equality-in equality-out" naturality squares, etc) will save you, because you get stuck on, like, funext λ _ → refl, so you need to add a rewrite rule for that.

and that's assuming you somehow manage to write both your category of renamings and your category of contexts to have definitional identity/associativity, and your embedding of renamings into contexts is definitionally functorial!)

the problem here is that while we know how to write composition in the fundamental fibration without transporting the relevant parts, and we know how to do that in a gluing, the generic change-of-base construction can't help but use the native subst. so you either give up on computation and commit to wading through the cruft, or you define gluings directly in components, and both of these feel like taking an L.

... or you can record, in the definition of the fundamental fibration, how to transport triangles without touching the underlying map; and in the definition of change-of-base, you use - and preserve - the preferred transport function. now concrete computation doesn't pick up any transports, not even transport refls that could compute away in book HoTT; and even in extremely generic cases where nothing can compute because everything is variables, your existing technique, whatever it may be, to reducing an equation to "the relevant parts", automatically drops any transports that were in the way.
